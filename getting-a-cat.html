Things to Consider Before Getting a Cat

Adopting a cat is a big commitment. Here are some factors to consider:

Consideration Description
Lifestyle Compatibility Think about your daily routine and whether a cat will fit into it. Some cats are independent, while others need more attention and interaction.
Allergies Check if anyone in your household is allergic to cats, as this can significantly impact your living environment.
Commitment Length Cats can live 15–20 years, so be prepared for a long-term commitment.
Other Pets Consider how other pets in your home will react to a new cat, and whether they’re friendly with other animals.
Housing Restrictions Ensure your housing allows pets, especially cats, as some rentals have restrictions.
Grooming Needs Some cats require regular grooming, particularly long-haired breeds, which may shed more and need brushing to prevent mats.
